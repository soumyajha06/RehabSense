🧠 AI Pipeline — Block Explanations

Here's what each block in the RehabSense AI pipeline does, explained simply for your presentation:
CNN Convolutional Neural Network — Spatial Block
The CNN block looks at each video frame like a picture. It detects body parts — joints, limbs, and their positions — by scanning for visual patterns (edges, shapes, contours). Think of it like eyes that identify "where is the knee in this frame?" It processes spatial (location-based) information. In RehabSense, CNN powers MediaPipe's pose detection to find 33 body landmark coordinates.
LSTM Long Short-Term Memory — Temporal Block
The LSTM block processes information across time — it remembers what happened in previous frames to understand movement. Where CNN sees a snapshot, LSTM understands a sequence. For example: it can tell the difference between a knee bending (flexion) vs extending (extension) by remembering the last 10–20 frames. It answers "how is this joint moving over time?"
Dense FC Fully Connected Layer — Decision Block
The Dense FC (Fully Connected) layer is the "thinking" part. It takes all the spatial + temporal features from CNN and LSTM and combines them to make decisions. Every neuron is connected to every feature, so it can learn complex relationships like "if the knee angle is X and the hip is Y, then the form is incorrect." This is where movement quality scoring happens.
Softmax Softmax Classifier — Output Block
The Softmax block converts raw scores into probabilities that add up to 100%. For example: "Good Form: 78%, Partial: 15%, Incorrect: 7%." It picks the most likely category and outputs it as the final classification. In RehabSense, this drives the ✅ Good / ⚠️ Partial / ❌ Incorrect rep quality labels you see in the dashboard.
📐 Flexion / Extension — Phase Detection
The Flexion phase is when the joint angle decreases — bending (like bending your knee). The Extension phase is when the joint angle increases — straightening (like straightening your knee). The phase bar at the bottom of the angle panel shows which phase you're currently in. A complete rep = one full flexion + one full extension cycle.
🎯 ROM — Range of Motion
ROM (Range of Motion) is the measurement of how far a joint can move from minimum to maximum angle in degrees (°). In rehabilitation, achieving the target ROM means the joint is healing and regaining function. The green zone on the angle bar shows the "Good ROM" target range for each joint. The score ring reflects how close the patient is to their target ROM.
